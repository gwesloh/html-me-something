It All Started With a Mouse

Our story, like so many others, started with a Mouse.

The Before

Elizabeth, my Wife, was born in Tucson, Arizona; and grew up in Hemosillo, Sonora in Northern Mexico. I grew up in Dellwood and Ferguson, Missouri.

Elizabeth was the youngest of three girls and attended a private all-girls school for kindergarten thru High School.

I have two older sisters and come from a large extended family, whom we see often.

I attended a coed private grade school in Ferguson followed by a private all-guys high school for a few years before finishing at a coed private high school.

I was born with a Disability, I had a stroke before birth and as a result I have spent my entire life with partial paralysis on the left side of my body. For most of my life and in most situations, I have attempted to ignore or draw attention away from my disability because discussing or confronting it is often awkward or uncomfortable, but in recent years I have begun to try for a more balanced approach of acknowledging my limitations while striving to overcome the negative stigma placed on disability in our culture. Due, in large part, to my amazing family, I have never felt truly helpless or trapped by my disability. Although it did take me much longer to learn some basic tasks like tying shoes or riding a bike.

Just before her senior year of high school in Hermosillo, Elizabeth was visiting her sister in Tucson. While in Tucson her family was faced with a difficult situation and Elizabeth was forced to get her GED and begin Community College instead of returning home.

At roughly the same time I was attending Community College in St. Louis where I was spending most of my time with my best friends. One of these friends, whom I had known since kindergarten, came to me one day and casually announced that she had decided to pursue an internship in Florida. I thought that sounded like a great idea and signed up shortly after.

The Happiest Place on Earth

This is where that Famous Mouse comes in.

My friend and I began our internships at Walt Disney World in Mid-January 2008. I was placed in a small fast food restaurant in one of the on-property resorts. As luck would have it, or Magic if you’re a Disneyphile, Elizabeth started an internship at the same restaurant a week or so later.

I would like to say it was love at first sight.

It wasn’t.

It took us awhile. I was pursuing another coworker of ours, and Elizabeth did not want a relationship. I had a car though and that was a rare commodity; and after I offered several times to drive her home (gentleman that I was) she accepted, and a friendship began. As we began spending more and more time together feelings began to develop, and I asked her out. She said No.

Our friendship continued and eventually she relented and agreed to date. It’s hard to be single at Disney. Romance and magic are everywhere and there is a reason it’s called the happiest place on earth.

We went on a date to Epcot. I remember thinking I was so smooth as we found a place to watch the fireworks. I mean, does it get more romantic than lakeside fireworks at Disney?

I asked if there was anything I could get her before the fireworks. She asked for a frozen lemonade. Easy enough.

I went to the stand just as they were closing, and the cast member said he did not have enough of either flavor for a full cup. I told him I was trying to impress a date and we agreed she would be wowed if I got a mix of both lemonade and raspberry lemonade so as not to return empty handed.

We were wrong, but it was still the best date of my life.

I would like to say, after all that, it was happily ever after.

It wasn’t.

We dated for most of our internship with a short break when I got scared of the idea of long distance dating after.

Long Distance Dating, Phoenix, Saint Louis and Marriage

After the internship, we tried the long-distance thing with me in St. Louis and Elizabeth in Tucson and had another short break. Then we both moved to Phoenix and started our life together.

Money was tight and after a while of working two jobs to put herself through college, Elizabeth was forced to move back to her family in Tucson. We took another break.

Finally, after college she moved back to Phoenix and I asked her to marry me. She said yes.

Shortly after, we moved to St. Louis near my family and got married before settling in Ferguson.

Parenthood and Our Goal

In September of 2016 we welcomed our beautiful daughter, Ainsley. We think she is very photogenic, as you will see.

In the years that have followed we have loved getting to know our daughter and watching her grow, and we hope that she will have siblings soon.

We both live with chronic depression and anxiety and each day can bring new challenges, but we try to face them together.

We are starting this blog not because we think we are expert parents, but because we know how tough it can be.

Elizabeth works full time and I recently left my job as a stock broker and was a SAHD before joining LaunchCode to learn coding. My sister and my parents take care of Ainsley.

Life can feel overwhelming at times. Budgeting is tough. Dieting is tough. Sometimes just staying sane is tough.

Physically, Mentally, Emotionally, Financially and in every other way Parenthood is Draining

In other words:

The Parenthood Struggle is real.

We are here to build a community of parents, grandparents, aunts, uncles, neighbors and friends to share tips, ideas, stories, products, motivations and what gets them through the day.
